Impact

The Harlem Renaissance has had a huge cultural influence around the world. Through multiple art forms, it became a catalyst for change. Being a part of the mainstream made it vulnerable to cultural appropriation. For better or worse, the impact to music and culture has been monumental. It laid the foundation for hip hop. Much like rap battles today, bands did battle in these clubs.

New York and Harlem became a music mecca that stands strong to this day. It went strong until the market crashed in 1929 and the subsequent Great Depression. That along with the end of prohibition put a damper on the growth of the night clubs. After the race riot of 1935, the optimism of the Harlem Renaissance and the idea that blacks could gain equality faded.
